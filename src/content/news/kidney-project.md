Diabetic kidney disease is a major complication for patients with diabetes. To combat this, **CQDM, Roche, The Jewish General Hospital Foundation, and McGill University** have partnered to fund a new, large-scale research initiative.

### The Goal of the Project
The primary objective of this project is to uncover multi-omic biomarkers that can predict the progression of diabetic nephropathy. By utilizing the BioPortal infrastructure, researchers will have access to:

* High-fidelity proteomics (Olink HT Explore panels)
* Comprehensive genomic sequencing
* Deeply phenotyped clinical data

> "This collaboration represents a massive step forward in precision medicine. By pooling our resources and leveraging open science, we can identify risks earlier and tailor treatments to individual patients."

All of these components are made in the same style, and can easily be integrated into projects, allowing researchers worldwide to create hundreds of solutions for future clinical applications.

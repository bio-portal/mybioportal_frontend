We are excited to announce that **BioPortal**, a comprehensive diabetes research cohort based at the Jewish General Hospital and affiliated with McGill University, is now officially accepting data access applications from researchers across the globe.

### What Makes BioPortal Unique?
BioPortal currently includes 6,000 participants living with Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes, with ongoing recruitment aiming to expand the cohort to 12,000 participants in the coming years. Notably, more than half of the participants are of non-European heritage, providing a highly diverse and real-world clinical dataset.

What truly sets this cohort apart is the unprecedented depth and breadth of the multi-modal data available:

* **Olink Proteomics (5400 HT):** High-throughput proteomics data for 1,500 participants, with an additional 1,500 becoming available for academic researchers within the year.
* **Retinal Imaging:** Comprehensive retinal imaging across the cohort.
* **Complete Medical Records:** Full longitudinal medical histories with ongoing data collection.
* **Laboratory & Medication Data:** Extensive lab test results and detailed medication tracking.
* **Advanced Imaging:** Multiple imaging modalities, with more actively being added.
* **Coming Soon:** Whole genome genotyping is currently underway.

### Who Can Apply?
BioPortal is designed to support the entire scientific community. We welcome applications from:
* **Academic Researchers** (Preferential rates/reduced access fees apply)
* **Industry Partners**
* **Organizations** interested in formal partnerships to help grow and enrich the dataset.

All applications require a standardized data access agreement and a nominal fee to support the infrastructure.
